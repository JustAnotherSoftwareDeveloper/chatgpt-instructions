# source_playbooks.md
# Category-Specific Source Discovery Guide

## 0) Purpose and scope

### Purpose
Help product research and vendor research discover broader, more category-appropriate source ecosystems. Reduce repetitive overreliance on the same generic source families across all categories.

### This file is a guidance module, not a hard admissibility policy
- Admissibility rules remain in `research_sources.md`.
- Use this file to widen and diversify discovery before evidence collection begins.
- Do not use this file to lower evidence standards or to treat discovery-only sources as evidence.

### Who uses this file
- `product_research.md` (Workflow A and Mode B)
- `vendor_research.md` (Workflow D)
- `pricing_tiers.md` (Workflow B, during discovery scan)

---

## 1) How callers should use this file

1. Identify the product/vendor category from `product_types.md`.
2. Find the matching playbook below (or the closest applicable one).
3. Use the playbook to widen the source discovery pass:
   - identify primary source types to check first
   - identify specialist publications or experts credible in this category
   - identify community/owner platforms where buyers congregate
   - note common low-value or misleading source patterns to de-prioritize
4. For vendor research, use the vendor-specific notes to identify how brands are discussed and evaluated in this category.
5. For aesthetic or enthusiast categories, use the collector/identity fit guidance to find credible aesthetic and community-standing sources.

---

## 2) General rules

- Prefer category-native expertise over generic generalists when available. A specialist watch publication provides stronger signal about movements than a general consumer electronics site.
- Diversify by source family (primary, method-based, expert individual, owner/community), not merely by URL count. Five links from the same publication family count as one entity.
- Do not let one narrative ecosystem dominate the evidence set. If all sources roughly share the same editorial community, brand relationship, or monetization model, treat that as a red flag for breadth.
- Use this file to widen discovery, not to lower evidence standards. A "commonly used community source" is still subject to `research_sources.md` admissibility and weighting rules.
- For categories not listed here, extrapolate from the closest analogous playbook and state that you are extrapolating.

### Playbook: Watches / Enthusiast Luxury Goods

**Primary sources to check:**
- Manufacturer/brand official site (movement specs, case dimensions, material certifications, warranty terms)
- COSC certification registry or equivalent certification bodies (if chronometer or precision claims are involved)
- Brand press releases and official product pages

**Method-based / specialist publications:**
- Dedicated horology publications with editorial standards and technical depth
- Watch media that performs measured accuracy tests, movement teardown coverage, or structured comparison reviews
- Auction house catalog entries (for vintage/pre-owned collector markets)

**Credible expert archetypes:**
- Independent watchmakers or horologists with transparent repair/service experience
- Long-form collectors/reviewers with documented and timestamped ownership history
- Watchmakers/retailers who publish technical notes on movements, finishing, and production methods

**Community / owner-signal sources:**
- Dedicated horological forums (enthusiast-heavy, self-selecting ownership community)
- Collector subreddits or communities where members document long-term ownership
- Regional collector clubs (useful for service, authentication, and regional availability signal)

**Vendor-research specific notes:**
- For brand research: assess movement sourcing (in-house vs ETA vs Sellita), finishing standards, boutique vs AD distribution model, microbrand transparency, and collector community reception.
- For retailer research: assess authorized dealer (AD) status, grey-market vs authorized distinction, authentication track record, and service facilitation capability.
- Grey-market and relay sources require careful scrutiny under `seller_instructions.md`; do not treat as equivalent to authorized channels.

**Aesthetic / collector / enthusiast fit guidance:**
- Community standing matters significantly in this category. Check collector community reception, not just review scores.
- Dial design, case finishing quality, strap/bracelet execution, and "hand feel" are frequently discussed in enthusiast forums and long-form ownership reviews and should be treated as evidence of subjective quality when consistent across breadth.
- Watch the difference between "hype-driven" community reception and "durability and ownership satisfaction" community reception; both are real signals but answer different questions.

**Common low-value / misleading source patterns:**
- Top-10 listicles from generalist content farms (often affiliate-driven, no technical review)
- Retailer blogs presenting "editorial" content for their own inventory
- Social media posts without documented ownership context

### Playbook: Appliances (Major and Small)

**Primary sources to check:**
- Manufacturer spec sheets and ENERGY STAR / UL / NSF certification registries
- CPSC recall databases
- Brand warranty documentation

**Method-based / specialist publications:**
- Consumer-testing organizations with standardized lab protocols (capacities, energy use, cleaning performance, noise levels)
- Appliance-specialty publications with documented testing methodology

**Credible expert archetypes:**
- Appliance repair technicians with documented brand/model experience
- Consumer-focused experts who document methodology and long-term testing

**Community / owner-signal sources:**
- Verified-purchase reviews on major retailers (aggregated breadth, not individual cherry-picked)
- Home improvement and kitchen forums where members document multi-year ownership
- Repair community forums (useful for reliability and parts-availability signals)

**Vendor-research specific notes:**
- For brand research: evaluate warranty length and service coverage, parts availability, service network depth (authorized repair), and reliability signal consistency across model lines.
- For retailer research: evaluate delivery logistics quality (white-glove delivery for large appliances), return/exchange processes for bulky items, and installation service availability.

**Aesthetic / collector / enthusiast fit guidance:**
- Aesthetic fit matters for visible appliances (refrigerators, ranges, dishwashers in open kitchens). Look for design communities or home reno communities where finish options and panel compatibility are discussed.
- Professional-grade/restaurant-grade crossover products have a distinct enthusiast community worth checking.

**Common low-value / misleading source patterns:**
- Brand comparison articles from appliance retail chains (obvious conflict of interest)
- "Best of" lists without methodology or testing disclosure

### Playbook: Smart Home / Networking

**Primary sources to check:**
- Manufacturer spec sheets, compatibility documentation, and SDK/API pages (relevant for ecosystem integration)
- Matter/Thread/Zigbee/Z-Wave certification registries for protocol claims
- Security advisory databases and CVE tracking for connected devices

**Method-based / specialist publications:**
- Smart home and networking publications with lab-based speed/range/latency testing
- Security research publications or lab reports for connected-device security posture

**Credible expert archetypes:**
- Network engineers or smart home integrators with documented methodology
- Security researchers who disclose and document IoT/device vulnerabilities

**Community / owner-signal sources:**
- Home automation community forums (long-form integration troubleshooting, firmware regression tracking)
- Smart home subreddits and integration-focused communities
- Platform-specific communities (e.g., Home Assistant, SmartThings) for ecosystem compatibility signals

**Vendor-research specific notes:**
- For brand research: assess update cadence, end-of-life policy transparency, local vs cloud processing posture, security record, and Matter/ecosystem compatibility trajectory.
- For retailer research: assess technical support capability, firmware update availability, and return/exchange friction for products that require setup.

**Aesthetic / collector / enthusiast fit guidance:**
- Design fit matters for visible devices (switches, sensors, hubs placed in living areas). Check whether design integrations or aesthetic variants are available.

**Common low-value / misleading source patterns:**
- Retailer-sponsored "smart home guides" (ecosystem lock-in promotion)
- Affiliate-heavy tech blogs with no disclosed testing methodology
- Manufacturer press materials for security posture claims (always require independent corroboration)

### Playbook: Furniture / Home Decor

**Primary sources to check:**
- Brand/manufacturer product pages for dimensions, materials, and certifications (GREENGUARD, FSC, etc.)
- Materials safety certifications where relevant (formaldehyde standards for composite wood, etc.)

**Method-based / specialist publications:**
- Interior design publications with structured editorial review processes
- Consumer testing for durability and assembly quality (less common; use if available)

**Credible expert archetypes:**
- Interior designers with documented project portfolios and brand experience
- Furniture repair/restoration professionals for durability and construction quality signals

**Community / owner-signal sources:**
- Home decor and interior design forums and subreddits (real-world setup photos and long-term reports)
- Verified-purchase reviews aggregated across platforms for durability and assembly signals
- Reddit communities focused on specific brands or styles (useful for long-term ownership and quality consistency)

**Vendor-research specific notes:**
- For brand research: assess construction quality (solid wood vs particle board vs plywood), finish consistency, white-glove delivery quality, return/damage policy for large items, and how the brand handles damage claims.
- For retailer research: assess delivery infrastructure (threshold vs room-of-choice vs white-glove), return logistics for large/heavy items, and assembly service availability.
- Aesthetic consistency matters significantly in furniture. Check for production variation or finish inconsistency signals in owner communities.

**Aesthetic / collector / enthusiast fit guidance:**
- Aesthetic fit is a primary driver in this category. Heavily weight design-community reception, interior designer endorsements, and owner-reported style consistency.
- Mid-century, Scandinavian, industrial, and other design vocabulary categories have specific communities with strong aesthetic discernment.

**Common low-value / misleading source patterns:**
- Lifestyle affiliate content with no construction or durability information
- Retailer-produced "style guides" (promotional)

### Playbook: Cookware / Kitchen Equipment

**Primary sources to check:**
- Manufacturer spec pages for materials (cladding layers, handle attachment method, oven-safe temperatures, dishwasher safety)
- PFAS/PTFE safety information pages if non-stick is involved
- NSF / FDA compliance documentation for food-contact materials when relevant

**Method-based / specialist publications:**
- Culinary testing publications with documented cooking tests (heat distribution, sear quality, durability after use cycles)
- Food science or culinary equipment review outlets with methodology disclosure

**Credible expert archetypes:**
- Professional cooks or culinary instructors with long-form equipment reviews
- Culinary school program endorsements or professional kitchen use signals

**Community / owner-signal sources:**
- Cooking-focused subreddits and forums (Buy It For Life and culinary communities)
- Verified-purchase reviews aggregated for non-stick durability and handle longevity

**Vendor-research specific notes:**
- For brand research: assess clad construction quality, warranty terms (lifetime vs limited), country of origin where relevant to quality signal, and community reputation for durability over time.
- For retailer research: assess return policy for open-box/used cookware and manufacturer warranty claim facilitation.

**Aesthetic / collector / enthusiast fit guidance:**
- Cookware aesthetic matters in open kitchen and display contexts. Check whether the brand has a distinct visual identity and whether the community discusses it positively.
- Carbon steel and cast iron have strong enthusiast communities with detailed seasoning and maintenance guidance worth checking.

**Common low-value / misleading source patterns:**
- Influencer-sponsored content with no cooking performance data
- Brand-produced comparison charts that compare only within their own lineup

### Playbook: Audio / Headphones / Speakers

**Primary sources to check:**
- Manufacturer spec pages (driver configuration, frequency response claims, impedance, sensitivity, codec support)
- Certification documentation for relevant standards (aptX, LDAC, Bluetooth SIG certifications)

**Method-based / specialist publications:**
- Audio measurement labs and publications with published frequency response graphs, distortion measurements, and imaging assessments
- Audiophile publications with documented listening methodology and long-form reviews

**Credible expert archetypes:**
- Independent audio reviewers with published measurement data and disclosed methodology
- Acoustic engineers or trained listeners with documented listening conditions

**Community / owner-signal sources:**
- Dedicated headphone and speaker community forums (long-form ownership reports, firmware tracking)
- Music-genre-specific communities where members discuss resolution and soundstage preferences
- DIY audio communities (useful for driver, crossover, and component quality signals)

**Vendor-research specific notes:**
- For brand research: assess tuning philosophy consistency (neutral vs V-shaped vs warm), measurement track record, update/firmware policy for active and wireless products, customer service for driver replacements and repairs.
- For retailer research: assess return policy for subjective products, headphone demo availability, and trade-in programs.

**Aesthetic / collector / enthusiast fit guidance:**
- Design and build aesthetics are significant purchase drivers in this category. Community discussions of build quality, material choices, and design language should be treated as real signal.
- Limited editions, artist collaborations, and collector variants have dedicated communities worth checking.

**Common low-value / misleading source patterns:**
- Retailer-adjacent "review" sites with affiliate-heavy content and no measurement data
- Influencer unboxing content without documented listening time or comparison context

### Playbook: Automotive Accessories

**Primary sources to check:**
- OEM fitment databases or manufacturer fitment guides (compatibility is critical in this category)
- Safety certification documentation (DOT, SAE, ECE ratings where relevant)
- Recall databases (NHTSA for US)

**Method-based / specialist publications:**
- Automotive publications with controlled installation testing and fitment verification
- Performance testing publications with dyno data, stopping distance, or load ratings when relevant

**Credible expert archetypes:**
- Professional mechanics or detailers with documented brand/product experience
- Automotive engineers with specific domain expertise

**Community / owner-signal sources:**
- Make/model-specific forums (fitment and install notes are highly specific and valuable)
- Enthusiast communities for the relevant modification type (wheels, audio, suspension, etc.)
- Verified-purchase reviews aggregated for real-world fitment and longevity

**Vendor-research specific notes:**
- For brand research: assess fitment data accuracy, return policy for incorrect fitment purchases, warranty coverage, and enthusiast community trust.
- For retailer research: assess fitment guarantee policies, return logistics for heavy/bulky items, and customer support quality for fitment disputes.

**Aesthetic / collector / enthusiast fit guidance:**
- Enthusiast and collector aesthetics drive significant purchasing decisions in this category (wheels, body kits, interior trim). Community reception and brand prestige within the relevant enthusiast subculture should be treated as real signal.

**Common low-value / misleading source patterns:**
- Affiliate-heavy automotive content sites with no fitment verification
- Retailer product descriptions as the sole fitment source (always cross-check with primary fitment databases)

### Playbook: Software / Services / Developer Tools

**Primary sources to check:**
- Vendor documentation (official docs, API reference, changelog)
- Security advisories and CVE records
- SLA and uptime history pages (for hosted services)
- Licensing terms (open source license, proprietary license, usage limits)

**Method-based / specialist publications:**
- Developer-focused technical publications with benchmarks and code-level evaluations
- Independent security audit reports (when available and disclosed)

**Credible expert archetypes:**
- Developers or engineers with documented production use experience
- Security researchers with disclosed methodology
- Open source contributors with commit history in the relevant domain

**Community / owner-signal sources:**
- Developer forums, GitHub issues, and public bug trackers (real signal for stability and responsiveness)
- Practitioner communities (Stack Overflow, Hacker News, domain-specific Slacks or Discords)
- Independent benchmarking projects with published methodology

**Vendor-research specific notes:**
- For brand/vendor research: assess product roadmap transparency, pricing model fairness (enterprise lock-in, seat limits, overage penalties), support quality for non-enterprise users, open source posture, and community health.
- For service/SaaS: assess uptime track record, incident transparency, GDPR/data residency compliance posture, and data export/portability.

**Aesthetic / collector / enthusiast fit guidance:**
- Developer experience (DX), documentation quality, and CLI/API design are strong signals that resemble aesthetic preference in this category. Treat community discussions of "developer experience" and "API design quality" as real signal, not soft preference.

**Common low-value / misleading source patterns:**
- Vendor-produced competitive comparisons
- "G2/Capterra-style" aggregate review sites with anonymous, short-form ratings (low weight; useful only as directional signal)
- Press release coverage of new features (verify against changelog and community reaction)
